1.

You could pay extra for every internet service

The EU is planning a regulation that will allow Internet providers to charge for every service extra money. They call it "specialised services" and its a way to sell you internet services that right know you don't have to pay for.
2.

Internet providers control what you can and can't do online

With this EU regulation Internet Providers will be able to block websites they concider illegal or harmfull and they do not even need a court order to do that. We don't need Internet providers to decide what we can and can't do online a free and open internet treats all content equaly.
3.

Freedom of speech would be restricted and innovation stifled

Right now big companies like Microsoft and Facebook are on the same level as our Blogs or Podcasts. When the EU Regulation is passed they will be on the fast lane on the data highway. Start Ups or non commercial websites like Wikpedia will be on the slow lane.
Net neutrality means that anyone can communicate with anyone, without discrimination and without asking for permission. This is why the internet has become such an important platform for social and cultural activity and democratic discourse. The EU is now planning to get rid of the neutral and open Internet. If we let this happen the Internet will become like cable TV and your operator can charge for every service you use. Big telecom companies should not decide what we can and can't do online. We have to keep the Internet neutral and open for everybody, so call your Parliamentarians to protect net neutrality.

famous critics

The critic on the proposed regulation came from many directions. First from the comission itself, even before the draft was publicly announced one part of the commission criticed the other because this proposal is dangerous for net neutrality.

The seond most critic is the Body of European Regulators of Electronic Communication (BEREC). This independend expert group of national regulators helped the objective discussion about net neutrality a lot and came up with many good definitionst (e.g. for specialised services) and statistical data that showed that half of europs population is already affected by net neutrality violations in the mobile sector. Also the European Data Protection Supervisor warns for loopholes in the net neutrality provisions and is highlighting the danger for the protection of our communication data in the new regime.

Civil Society has been watching this EU regulation very closely. You can find a great overview about the Dossier at Access Now, European Digital Rights and La Quadratur du Net.
